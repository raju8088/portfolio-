Brutal Honesty
WHAT THEY
DON'T TELL YOU.

✓ What Works

  • Niche specificity beats breadth every time. The GST tool for 500 CAs beats a generic AI assistant for 50,000 users.
  • Distribution is the moat, not the AI. Your email list and YouTube SEO are harder to copy than your tech stack.
  • Agency cash funds product build. Use high-ticket services to fund the SaaS. Don't try to bootstrap both simultaneously.
  • ₹25CR is a 18-24 month goal. Month 1 goal: ₹1L. Stay boring, stay specific.
  • Faceless works because trust flows to the brand name, not the person. Build lore around the brand, not you.

✗ What Kills It

  • Picking too broad a niche. "AI for Indian businesses" is a cemetery. "AI for D2C founders doing ₹1–10CR revenue" is a goldmine.
  • Automating before validating. Build manually for first 10 clients, then automate what's working.
  • Neglecting distribution. The best AI tool with no email list is invisible. Build the list from Day 1.
  • Zero retention strategy. SaaS dies without onboarding. Automate the first 30 days of user experience obsessively.
  • Treating this like a side project. At ₹25CR scale, this is a business. Register a company, get proper accounting, pay your taxes.
